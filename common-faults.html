Common Boiler Faults

Quick tips and professional guidance for the most frequent heating system issues.

Typical Issues Homeowners Face

Before calling an engineer, you may be able to identify the problem using the information below. Always refer to your boiler’s manual before attempting any reset or pressure adjustments.

Low Water Pressure

Check your boiler’s pressure gauge. It should read between 1.0 and 1.5 bar. If it’s lower, you may need to top up using the filling loop as described in your boiler manual.

Frozen Condensate Pipe

In cold weather, the external condensate pipe may freeze. Pour warm (not boiling) water over the pipe and reset your boiler once thawed.

No Heating or Hot Water

Check thermostat settings, power supply, and system pressure. If the issue persists, contact a Gas Safe engineer to inspect the diverter valve or ignition components.

Radiators Not Heating Evenly

Cold spots may indicate trapped air or sludge buildup. Bleed your radiators or consider a powerflush to improve system performance.

Boiler Keeps Turning Off

This can be caused by low pressure, a faulty thermostat, or blocked condensate pipe. Check for error codes and contact an engineer if unsure.
