Common Boiler Faults

Quick tips for simple issues – but always seek help if you’re unsure.

Basic Boiler Faults & Solutions

Below are some of the most common issues homeowners face. These tips may help you identify or fix simple problems, but if you’re unsure, always contact a Gas Safe Registered engineer.

Low Water Pressure

If your boiler’s pressure gauge is below 1 bar, it might need topping up. Check your boiler manual to locate the filling loop and carefully increase pressure to 1.2 bar, then reset the boiler.

No Heating or Hot Water

This could be due to a faulty thermostat, motorised valve, or airlock. Try resetting your thermostat and checking radiator valves are open before calling an engineer.

Kettling Sounds

If your boiler sounds like a kettle boiling, limescale or sludge could be restricting water flow. A professional powerflush can fix this problem.

Pilot Light Won’t Stay On

Ensure gas supply is on and check the flame sensor. If the pilot light still won’t ignite, do not attempt further repairs — call a Gas Safe engineer.

Leaking Boiler

Leaks may come from corroded components or loose joints. Turn off the water supply and call an engineer immediately to prevent damage.

Cold Radiators

Try bleeding radiators to remove trapped air. If some still stay cold, there may be sludge in the system — consider a powerflush service.
